#excerpt: 'We learn through daily experiences to associate the felt softness of an object with the visual deformation that occurs when we interact with it. However, it remains unclear how these visual and haptic cues are combined to help us estimate the deformability of an object and thes cues are weighted. To better understand the integration of visual and haptic cues, a grasping task that introduced a sensory conflict using a visual environment was developed. Here, preliminary results validated the experimental paradigm and open new possibilities to explore how people compare the reliability of visual and tactile cues"
